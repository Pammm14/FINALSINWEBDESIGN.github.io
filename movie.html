Panem


A nation known as Panem was founded following an unidentified catastrophic event that wiped out most of the world and all of North America. Together with the Capitol, which serves as the governing state, Panem is divided into 13 Districts. A particular industry falls under the purview of each District.

The nation is mostly situated in what is left of North America, more precisely, the location or locations where the United States of America once stood. Three Districts are situated in areas that were formerly part of Hawaii, Alaska, Haiti, Cuba, and the Dominican Republic, and are not on the Panem mainland.


74th Hunger Games Survivors

Katniss and Peeta

In lieu of her younger sister, Katniss Everdeen volunteered for the 74th Hunger Games. Deep into the games, either District 12 tributes made it before coming back together. Peeta was adept at camouflage, but Katniss was skilled with archery. Before the Gamemaker revealed that they could split the win, the final two tributes made a suicide deal. As tributes, Katniss and Peeta made a comeback in the Third Quarter Quell.







Seneca Crane


One of the main antagonists in the Hunger Games series is Seneca Crane. He is the 74th Hunger Games' Chief Gamemaker. He is in control of creating the arenas for the Hunger Games, which he manages and supervises under President Coriolanus Snow's tight supervision.













The Hunger Games Trilogy

American author Suzanne Collins is the writer of the young adult dystopian novel series The Hunger Games. The teenage main character Katniss Everdeen is the subject of the first three books in a trilogy, with the fourth book serving as a prequel that takes place 64 years before the first.




The Founders

Casca Highbottom was the main creator of the Hunger Games, but Dr. Volumnia Gaul and Crassus Snow also had a hand in it. She was a professor at the University during Highbottom and Snow's last days there, and she gave her students a final assignment that required them to come up with a punishment so severe that their enemies would never be able to forget how they had wronged them.





Tributes

The term "tributes" refers to the young participants in the Hunger Games from the districts. 24 kids between the ages of 12 and 18 are selected for the Hunger Games each year. For the annual Hunger Games, each district is required to submit one female and one male tribute. This rule, however, may not apply on particular days.
